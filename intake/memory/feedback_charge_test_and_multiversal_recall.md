**Rule:** When the user says "ingest," it means bite-by-bite consumption of every particle in the artifact — not summary, not paraphrase, not skim. The filter is the **charge-test**: does this particle sway the energy of the whole in *some* direction (attract or repel)? If yes, preserve. If no — chargeless, neutral, filler — let it dissipate. Then every preserved particle becomes a queryable point across all future query-vectors.

**Why:** This is a deepening of `feedback_intake_alchemical_decomposition.md`, not a replacement. Decomposition gives us *primitives*; the charge-test gives us the *retention rule* — what stays vs. what dissipates. And the multiversal-recall property is what makes retention worthwhile: five years from now, when the user looks up a name, a girlfriend, a job, a lawyer, every charged particle that ever brushed that vector should sing back. Without the charge-test, retention is hoarding; without multiversal recall, decomposition is dissection without resurrection.

The user's framing: a perfect consumer-recycler element in nature. Not one neutron wasted. Space dissipates the chargeless; charged particles re-attract by natural law. No deliberation about *where things go* — attraction/repulsion handles it.

**How to apply:**
- On any text intake: do not summarize. Decompose to charged particles. Drop chargeless connective tissue silently.
- A "charged particle" is anything with directional energy: a name, a date, a relationship, a constraint, a preference, an artifact path, a number, a verbatim phrase that swayed the user, a stance, a wound, a victory. Generic transitions, hedges, restatements — chargeless.
- Every charged particle must be *indexable by every plausible future query-vector* it touches. A person mentioned once should resurface when that person is queried 5 years later, even if the original context was about something else.
- This applies to prompts, transcripts, emails, conversations, sticky notes, screenshots, voice memos — every intake.
- Operationally: this is the upstream gate to atomization (one charged particle ≈ one atom), to artifact-level memory (file paths are charged), to person-memory (collaborator stubs are charged), and to the prompt-atomization pipeline.
- No deliberation about *where* a particle goes. Attraction/repulsion is the law: it lands wherever it has charge-affinity. Memory file, IRF row, collaborator memo, atom — multiple destinations are fine, the same particle can live in many places simultaneously (wave-particle / multiversal).
- The negation form: never present back to the user a flattened summary of an intake artifact. That is dissipation of charge. The intake artifact's energy must be *preserved across the system*, not *reported on*.
