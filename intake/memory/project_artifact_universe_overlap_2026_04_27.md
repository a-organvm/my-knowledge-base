## §5 — What I think (the user asked)

### The pattern
Both projects sit at the same phase: substrate built, audience-facing layer being shipped, blockers on third-party tools (CI token / Kit API / Discord provisioning). That phase-symmetry is the deepest evidence of universe-overlap — they're not just adjacent, they're *parallel*. The macro template (just shipped) is the formalization of that parallelism.

### The contribution flow is asymmetric
Rob → Maddie has more concrete methods (Bridge Content, 4-level funnel, warm-clock, Discord rituals) — Rob's universe has built more *generalizable* substrate. Maddie → Rob is more about *ontological depth* (sovereignty, persona refresh, naming-chains lenses). This asymmetry isn't a problem; it's the natural shape of two projects that started from different directions and now meet in the middle.

### The biggest leverage move
**Fill the 8-strata Domain Schema for both, separately.** It's already built (PRT-048 v2.2). Fill it for Maddie (sovereignty ontology made formal), fill it for Rob (Hokage allusions made formal). Once filled, the schema reveals what each universe *should* contribute to the other (constellation lane = explicit cross-references; internal-magnet vs external-contribution = bidirectional contribution map).

### The vacuum that matters most
**Maddie has no cadence.** Rob has a 3-cadence warm clock spec (Phase A unrun, but spec exists). Maddie has iMessage with no rhythm. Without cadence, M's L9 (asks-from-her) ages out and the relationship becomes reactive instead of paced. Fix this BEFORE shipping more deliverables. Cost: low. Leverage: high.

### What NOT to bring in
Don't import the chess-equipment sponsorship lane to Maddie's universe (wrong audience). Don't import the wellness-affiliate model to Rob (wrong vector). The macro's specific-vs-universal distinction (per macro §5) holds: lanes universal, contents specific.

### What I'd flag as the bridge artifact mystery
The "the gamified life" Drive doc appearing in both clusters is either (a) genuine bridge content (chess as sovereignty practice; sovereignty as gamified self-development) or (b) a misfile. Per the macro's drift-detection: open it, classify it, never let bridge artifacts sit silently overlapping. If (a), it's a *named bridge* and both universes should reference it. If (b), unfile it and pick its real home.

### What I'd add for the next person you bring in
Before onboarding Scott / Jessica / other, do this: take the universe-overlap §3 + §4 lists and pre-screen the new person against them. *Which existing methods will they import? Which new methods might they contribute?* This pre-screen answers "where do they fit in the universe" before the trail file even gets written.
