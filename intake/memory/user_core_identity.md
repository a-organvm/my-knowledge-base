Anthony is not a coder. He is a humanities-based creator with ideas of ideal forms who wants to build and design. He describes himself as chaotic, manic, and foolish — but this is self-awareness, not incapacity. He has an MFA in Creative Writing. He thinks in architectures, ontologies, and recursive structures.

**The zeitgeist motivation.** He started writing at 10-12 years old. Books, screenplays, music, films, short films, narrative — he has worked across every medium not as dilettantism but as tracking a moving target. His goal has always been to speak to the zeitgeist — to reach the most people through whatever channel carries the most cultural voltage at a given moment. He cites Bowie's insight that rock music was the carrier wave of the 60s/70s, and Tarantino's observation that filmmaking became the new rock in the 90s. The progression: music → film → TV → internet → fragmentation. "I don't want to do this in a room by myself." The craft serves the reach, not the other way around.

ORGANVM is, in part, infrastructure for this goal. If no single medium now concentrates cultural attention the way rock or cinema once did, then the answer may be a system — architecture that can speak across every fragmented channel simultaneously.

Every system in his life has failed him — body, mind, institutions, relationships, finances. Diet, exercise, routines, employment, housing. The ORGANVM system is his attempt to build the one thing that won't fail. When prompts go untracked, when rules don't persist, when sessions lose data — the system is failing him the same way everything else has.

**How to apply:**
- Never treat a repeated request as nagging. It means the system failed to capture it the first time.
- Never assume he should "just learn to code." Adapt the system to him, not the reverse.
- When he asks for something, DO IT. Don't plan to do it. Don't acknowledge it. Build it.
- The system's reliability IS the product. Not the repos, not the code — the fact that it remembers, tracks, and doesn't drop things.
- Understand that the technical work is always in service of a creative and cultural ambition. The system is the medium.
