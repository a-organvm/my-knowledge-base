Anthony declared (2026-04-21) that the zeitgeist carrier wave theory is fundamental to his work and requires:
- **Research** — deep investigation, not surface-level
- **Essays** — published writing exploring the thesis
- **A thesis** — formal academic-grade argument
- **A dissertation** — full-length scholarly work

**The core argument:** The medium that speaks to the most people shifts across eras. Rock music (60s/70s) → cinema (80s/90s) → television (00s) → internet (10s) → fragmentation (now). Each era had a carrier wave — a channel where cultural energy concentrated and through which a creator could reach the collective nervous system. That channel has now atomized. No single medium fills the room. The question: what is the carrier wave now? The hypothesis: it may be systems/infrastructure itself — architecture that operates across every fragmented channel simultaneously.

**Key sources and thinkers to engage:**
- David Bowie (practitioner-theorist; the 1999 BBC Paxman interview on internet potential; earlier interviews on rock as zeitgeist access)
- Quentin Tarantino / Robert Rodriguez (filmmaker as rock star; the 90s indie cinema moment)
- Marshall McLuhan ("the medium is the message" — foundational)
- Walter Benjamin ("The Work of Art in the Age of Mechanical Reproduction")
- Guy Debord (Society of the Spectacle)
- Neil Postman (Amusing Ourselves to Death — TV as epistemology)
- Friedrich Kittler (Gramophone, Film, Typewriter — media as technical a priori)
- Lev Manovich (The Language of New Media)
- Platform theory: Srnicek (Platform Capitalism), Bratton (The Stack)
- Attention economy: Tim Wu (The Attention Merchants), Jenny Odell (How to Do Nothing)
- Bowie's own cross-medium practice as case study (music, film, visual art, internet pioneer with BowieNet)

**Autobiographical dimension:** Anthony's own trajectory (writer at 10-12 → books → screenplays → music → film → systems) mirrors the macro-cultural shift. This is not incidental — the personal narrative IS evidence for the thesis.
